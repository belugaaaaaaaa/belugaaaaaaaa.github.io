Front-page narrative

What Ming Official History Displays First

The homepage is deliberately rich: emperors are easy to browse, reforms are easy to admire, and the state's achievements are easy to index.

Founders

Zhu Yuanzhang and the founding narrative receive clarity, sequence, and purpose.

Expansion

Yongle's court, fleets, and capital rebuilding appear as legible acts of will.

Reform

Elite administrators are remembered as authors of order rather than managers of extraction.

Prosperity

Commercial vitality appears as surface brilliance, not as the labor of millions.

Buried navigation

What the Archive Marginalizes

Labor is not given the same typographic dignity as court history. The hierarchy of navigation mirrors the hierarchy of memory.
