Palace Construction

Seat of Renewal

The official record celebrates architectural grace, courtly order, and imperial magnificence.

Open counter-record
Thousands of laborers moved stone, timber, and ceremonial materials.
Deaths entered as work loss totals; names largely absent.
Primary trace: quarry payrolls and injury notices, not biographies.
Canal Building

Artery of Commerce

The state narrative praises integration, efficiency, prosperity, and engineering foresight.

Open counter-record
Diggers, haulers, and temporary camp workers appear as grouped tallies.
Forced and seasonal labor blurred together in official summaries.
Oral testimonies absent from preserved state files.
Railway Expansion

Line of Modernization

National maps frame rail construction as inevitable progress and strategic cohesion.

Open counter-record
Track-laying crews counted by labor unit, not by person.
Accident reports survive; many identities do not.
Women providing food, washing, and supply work remain peripheral to the file.
Wartime Production

National Emergency Output

Official exhibits focus on patriotic efficiency, industrial discipline, and strategic necessity.

Open counter-record
Workers survive in charts, quotas, and output graphs.
Factory illness and injury were often treated as administrative costs.
Most labor remains recoverable only as quantity.
